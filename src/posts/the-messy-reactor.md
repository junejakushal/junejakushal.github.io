Richard Feynman spent a summer in college as the entire chemistry department of a company called Metaplast Corporation. Their product was metal-plated plastic. Their problem was getting the plating to stick. His title was Chief Research Chemist. His staff was himself.

Metaplast ran a full-page ad in Modern Plastics magazine. Chrome-bright photographs of their best work. A serious operation, to all appearances.

Years later, Feynman met a researcher who had been working in England on the same problem. The British lab had seen the Metaplast ads and shut down their own program. No point competing, they figured. Metaplast clearly had dozens of chemists, a whole department, a chief research chemist with his own office.

The whole department was a college student on summer break.

---

When Feynman arrived at Princeton for his PhD, he expected it to be grander than MIT. Better equipment. More elegant experiments. A place that looked like serious science was supposed to look. Instead the lab was smaller. The equipment was cobbled together. Everything was inelegant. Fiddling was the operative mode.

His reaction, in his own words: "that was the secret. So I loved Princeton right away."

The mess was not a failure of resources. It was a sign that something real was happening. You do not fiddle with what you already know. The improvised setup meant people were still asking questions the clean setup had stopped asking.

---

I cleared CFA Level 1 in the Feb 2026 window. I am not sitting for Level 2.

That is not a complaint about the curriculum. The curriculum is good. It is internally consistent in a way that most things are not. It knows what it is.

The problem is what it assumes.

The CFA's world has three years of audited financials. It has management that communicates clearly with analysts. It has receivables that are real, inventory that is counted, margins that someone has actually calculated. It has data that behaves.

I spent several months with an MSME in Jamshedpur where the production records lived in Excel files that someone consolidated by hand each morning. I sat in CFO conversations, which I wrote about in [Spinning Plates, Different Kitchens](/blog/spinning-plates-different-kitchens), where the same phrase meant completely different things depending on which balance sheet it came from. I watched distribution happen through instinct and trust and a rep who had been on the same route for years, which I wrote about in [The Plate is Wobbling](/blog/the-plate-is-wobbling).

The Damodaran model was right there in the room. Nobody had the inputs.

Level 2 would make me more fluent in a language the terrain does not speak. More rigorous inside the framework. Better at the exam. But the framework's assumptions are already the constraint. Going deeper into them is not the same as going closer to the ground.

---

The British lab had the proper setup. They looked at the Metaplast ad and saw what a serious operation was supposed to look like and stood down. The serious operation was one person who did not know what he was doing yet, fiddling until something stuck.

Level 2 is the proper lab. More elegance. More internal consistency. A more impressive ad in the magazine.

I would rather stay in the inelegant setup for a while. Sit with more businesses where the data is a guess and the judgment is everything. Do more live projects like the one in Adityapur, which I have been writing about in [Notes from Adityapur](/blog/notes-from-adityapur) and [The Pipe and the Pit](/blog/the-pipe-and-the-pit), where the interesting problems turn out to be a pipe layout and a certification definition and a founder making a decision in real time without opening a deck.

Watch more plates wobble.

In [Prepared to Play](/blog/prepared-to-play) I wrote about preparation as the thing that makes genuine curiosity possible. The CFA was preparation. It gave me enough of the language to follow a live conversation about cost of capital in real time, to ask a follow-up question about covenant buffers rather than asking what a covenant is. That is real and I do not want to minimise it.

But there is a difference between preparation that gives you the surplus to play and preparation that becomes its own destination. Level 2 starts to feel like the latter. More runway inside the framework rather than more altitude above it.

The curriculum will still be there when the terrain demands it. Right now the messy reactor is where the energy is. The wobbling is not a problem to be solved. The wobbling is the information.
